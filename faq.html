Platform as a Service (PaaS)

What is Platform as a Service (PaaS) and how does it differ from traditional hosting?

Platform as a Service (PaaS) provides a complete development and deployment environment in the cloud. Unlike traditional hosting where you manage the underlying infrastructure, PaaS allows developers to focus solely on building applications without worrying about servers, storage, or networking.

Key advantages: Faster time to market, reduced operational complexity, automatic scaling, and built-in development tools.

What programming languages and frameworks are supported by your PaaS solution?

Our PaaS supports a wide range of programming languages including Java, Python, Node.js, Ruby, PHP, Go, and .NET. We also provide support for popular frameworks like Spring Boot, Django, Express.js, Ruby on Rails, and Laravel.

Additionally, we offer container support for custom runtime environments, allowing you to deploy applications using Docker containers.

How is pricing calculated for PaaS services?

Our PaaS pricing is based on a combination of factors including compute resources, memory allocation, storage capacity, and outbound data transfer. We offer tiered pricing plans suitable for development, testing, and production environments.

You can start with our free tier for small projects and scale up as your needs grow. Detailed pricing information is available in our pricing calculator.

Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)

How does Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) provide more control than other cloud models?

IaaS gives you the highest level of control among cloud service models. You manage the operating systems, storage, deployed applications, and networking components, while we handle the physical hardware.

IaaS is ideal for: Companies needing full control over their infrastructure, organizations with specific compliance requirements, and businesses running custom applications that aren't compatible with PaaS environments.

What security measures are in place for your IaaS offerings?

Our IaaS security follows a shared responsibility model. We secure the underlying infrastructure including physical data centers, network, and hypervisors. You're responsible for securing your operating systems, applications, and data.

We provide advanced security features like DDoS protection, network firewalls, encryption at rest and in transit, identity and access management, and security monitoring tools.

What types of virtual machines and configurations are available?

We offer a wide range of VM configurations optimized for different workloads:

  • General purpose: Balanced CPU-to-memory ratio
  • Compute optimized: High CPU performance
  • Memory optimized: High memory-to-CPU ratio
  • Storage optimized: High disk throughput
  • GPU instances: For graphic-intensive workloads

All VMs can be customized based on your specific requirements.

Function as a Service (FaaS)

What are the main benefits of using Function as a Service (FaaS) for application development?

FaaS, or serverless computing, offers several key benefits:

  • Cost efficiency: You only pay for the compute time you consume
  • Automatic scaling: Functions scale automatically with demand
  • Reduced operational overhead: No server management required
  • Faster development: Focus on code rather than infrastructure

FaaS is perfect for event-driven applications, APIs, and processing tasks that have variable workloads.

What execution environments and runtimes are supported for serverless functions?

Our FaaS platform supports multiple programming languages including Node.js, Python, Java, Go, Ruby, and .NET Core. You can also bring custom runtimes using container images.

Each function execution is isolated in a secure environment with configurable memory allocation (128MB to 10GB) and timeout settings (up to 15 minutes).

Information Management as a Service (IMaaS)

How does Information Management as a Service (IMaaS) help with data governance?

Our IMaaS solutions provide comprehensive data governance capabilities including:

  • Data classification and cataloging
  • Policy management and enforcement
  • Data lineage tracking
  • Access controls and audit trails
  • Compliance reporting for regulations like GDPR, HIPAA, and CCPA

This ensures your data is managed consistently across the organization while meeting regulatory requirements.

What data encryption and protection measures are implemented?

We implement multiple layers of data protection:

  • Encryption at rest using AES-256
  • Encryption in transit with TLS 1.2+
  • Key management with customer-controlled keys
  • Data masking and tokenization options
  • Automated backup and point-in-time recovery

All security measures are compliant with industry standards and certifications.

Backend as a Service (BaaS)

What backend functionalities are included in your Backend as a Service (BaaS) offering?

Our BaaS provides a comprehensive set of backend services that mobile and web developers can integrate via APIs, including:

  • User authentication and management
  • Cloud storage and databases
  • Push notifications
  • Social media integration
  • Analytics and monitoring
  • Serverless cloud functions

This allows frontend developers to build full-featured applications without backend development expertise.

How is BaaS pricing structured?

BaaS pricing is based on usage metrics including:

  • Number of monthly active users
  • API calls and data transfer
  • Storage consumption
  • Push notifications sent

We offer a generous free tier for development and small projects, with transparent pricing as you scale.

General & Security

How do you ensure high availability and disaster recovery across your service offerings?

We employ a multi-layered approach to ensure high availability:

  • Geographically distributed data centers with automatic failover
  • 99.9% uptime SLA for all services
  • Automated backups with point-in-time recovery
  • Real-time replication across availability zones
  • Comprehensive monitoring and alerting systems

Our disaster recovery plans are regularly tested to ensure business continuity in case of major incidents.

What pricing models do you offer for your cloud services?

We offer flexible pricing models to suit different business needs:

  • Pay-as-you-go: Ideal for variable workloads with no long-term commitment
  • Reserved instances: Significant discounts for predictable, steady-state workloads
  • Spot pricing: Up to 90% discount for interruptible workloads
  • Enterprise agreements: Custom pricing for large-scale deployments

All our services include a free tier for testing and development purposes.
